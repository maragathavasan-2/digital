Jawaharlal Nehru

Jawaharlal Nehru was the first Prime Minister of India, serving from 1947 to 1964. He was a central figure in Indian politics before and after independence, and he played a key role in the Indian independence movement. Nehru was a staunch advocate of socialism, secularism, and democracy, and his policies helped shape the modern Indian state.

Early Life and Education

Born on November 14, 1889, in Allahabad, British India, Nehru was educated in England, studying at Harrow and Cambridge University. He was deeply influenced by the teachings of Mahatma Gandhi, and he became an active participant in the Indian freedom struggle.

Political Career

After India gained independence in 1947, Nehru became the country's first Prime Minister. He implemented various economic and social reforms and played a significant role in shaping India's foreign policy. Nehru's leadership was marked by his vision of a modern, industrialized, and democratic India.
